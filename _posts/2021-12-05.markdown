We find too much comfort in self-reproach. I feel much more noble when I punish myself, because perhaps then I'll become immune to the scrutiny of others. I've already picked out all there is about myself. So how can they punish me then? It constantly feels like a chore. But I'm not any more noble for being harsh on myself. Yet, I cannot stop. It's easy to default to self-hatred, when I have nothing else to pick at. Like somehow blaming myself would ease the burden I place on others. Maybe I just want to be told that I am alright-it's indeed not my fault, or even better, be told that harming myself would enable me to get my results the way I want it. Instead of doing it the hard way, which is to actually put in the effort to become better. Yet... yet... so many yet's and I still won't yet.
